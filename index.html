Asset Management Competency Framework for Canadian Communities

The Asset Management Competency Framework is designed to help Canadian communities develop and maintain the necessary skills and knowledge for effective asset management. This framework outlines the core competencies required for professionals involved in planning, acquiring, operating, maintaining, and disposing of public infrastructure assets.

Key Competency Areas

  • Strategic Planning: Developing long-term asset management plans aligned with community goals.
  • Financial Management: Understanding the financial aspects of asset management, including budgeting and funding.
  • Technical Expertise: Applying technical knowledge to asset maintenance and renewal.
  • Data Management: Collecting, analyzing, and using asset data for informed decision-making.
  • Risk Management: Identifying and mitigating risks associated with asset performance.
  • Stakeholder Engagement: Communicating and collaborating with stakeholders on asset management issues.

Benefits of the Framework

By adopting this framework, communities can improve their asset management practices, leading to better infrastructure performance, reduced costs, and enhanced service delivery. It also promotes a consistent approach to asset management across different communities.
